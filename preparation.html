Stepping stones for success

  • Build your resume:

      1.Make sure that your resume is job specific -

      Read the job description before you write your resume so that it helps you to choose the right keywords to include in your your resume

      2.Include contact information -

      First section of your resume should state all of your contact information, including your name, address (or city and state), phone number and email address. You can also include your social media profile links like github,linkedin and also personal blogs or personal portfolios links if any.

      3.Short summary about yourself -

      To make your own summary which includes information like who you are, what are your career goals and why you are the best candidate for the position you are applying for.

      4.Highlight your skills -

      Technical skills and soft skills both are to be included. It can be one or two column formats. Mention your skills according to the job description you are applying to, so that you can have a high chance of getting hired.

      5.Highlight education,training,certifications -

      Highlight the most relevant education, training and certifications you have. This could include your major and minor in college, professional license and certifications, certificate courses taken in college and additional professional training.

      6.Include relevant experience -

      Your internship experience or any volunteering experience which adds value to your profile.

      7.Highlight your interests and hobbies

      Mention the things that you like to do in free time.

      8.Proofread -

      Check whether your resume stands out with the job description you have applied to. You can also take help from your friends to get suggestions on your resume.

      1.Keep your resume short - Maximum 2 pages.
      2.AI is used nowadays to scan your resume so rather than making a two column format page, try to make it sequentially.
      3.Highlight your willingness to learn

    1. Brush up your CS subjects:

      List out the subjects that you have already read in your semester in a piece of paper and tag it as strong,need to revise,start from scratch.Need to revise are the ones you need to concentrate and start revising. The ones which are marked 'start from scratch' are needed to be learnt through tutorials or docs. Accordingly make a time-table for yourself.

      Try to practice initially using notepad, online compilers or respective IDE. You have websites like Hackerrank,Hackerearth,Codechef,Leetcode to practise.

    3. Learn Aptitude:

      Quantitative Aptitude Indiabix is a great website to practice aptitude.Topics you need to prepare: Permutations and Combination, Percentages, Profit and loss, Probability, Time and work, Simple and compound interest, Time, speed and distance, Number systems, Ages, Averages, Calender and clock.

      Verbal Ability- Topics to prepare: Synonyms, Antonyms, Reading Comprehension, Articles, Speech, Conjunctions, Tenses and Voices, Sentence Selection, Sentence Correction, Para Jumbles, etc.

      Logical Reasoning: The topics under logical reasoning are: Coding Decoding, Sitting Arrangements, Blood Relations, Clocks, Puzzle, Series, Syllogism, Cubes and Dices, Analogy Classification.

    4. Build your communication skills:

      Work on your reading skills, writing skills, and spoken English skills and become an excellent communicator. These skills will help you a lot in your professional career ahead. Don't let poor communication skills be a roadblock in your professional journey. Master them and use them to your growth and advantage.
